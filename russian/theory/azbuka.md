# Russian Azbuka (Азбука) — The Cyrillic Alphabet

## Overview

The Russian alphabet has **33 letters** (10 vowels, 21 consonants, 2 signs).
Good news for Slovak speakers: several letters look similar to Latin letters!

---

## Letters — Grouped by Familiarity

### 🟢 Easy: Look AND Sound Like Latin
| Cyrillic | Sounds like | Example | Translation |
|----------|------------|---------|-------------|
| А а | "a" in *father* | арбуз | watermelon |
| Е е | "ye" in *yes* | ель | spruce |
| О о | "o" in *pot* | ок | OK |
| К к | "k" in *king* | кот | cat |
| М м | "m" in *mother* | мама | mom |
| Т т | "t" in *top* | там | there |

### 🟡 Tricky: Look Like Latin but Sound Different!
| Cyrillic | Looks like | Actually sounds like | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| В в | B | "v" in *voice* | вода (water) |
| Н н | H or N | "n" in *no* | нет (no) |
| Р р | P | "r" (trilled r) | рыба (fish) |
| С с | C | "s" in *sun* | сок (juice) |
| У у | Y | "oo" in *moon* | утро (morning) |
| Х х | X | "kh" (like ch in *Bach*) | хлеб (bread) |

### 🔵 New: No Latin Equivalent
| Cyrillic | Sounds like | Example | Mnemonic |
|----------|------------|---------|----------|
| Б б | "b" in *boy* | брат | Looks like a 6 — 6 brothers |
| Г г | "g" in *go* | год | Looks like a hooked cane |
| Д д | "d" in *dog* | дом | Looks like a house (дом = house) |
| Ж ж | "zh" (like *s* in *measure*) | жить | Looks like a beetle — it buzzes |
| З з | "z" in *zoo* | зима | Looks like the number 3 |
| И и | "ee" in *see* | имя | Backwards N — like a mirrored N |
| Й й | "y" (short) in *boy* | йог | И with a breve — short И |
| Л л | "l" in *love* | луна | Looks like Λ (lambda) |
| П п | "p" in *pen* | папа | Looks like a doorway П |
| Ф ф | "f" in *fun* | фото | Looks like a φ (phi) |
| Ц ц | "ts" in *bits* | цирк | Looks like Ц with a foot |
| Ч ч | "ch" in *church* | чай | Looks like the number 4 |
| Ш ш | "sh" in *shop* | школа | Looks like a comb — 3 teeth |
| Щ щ | "shch" (longer sh) | щи | Like Ш with a tail |
| Ъ ъ | hard sign (no sound) | съезд | Makes prior consonant hard |
| Ы ы | like "i" in *bit* (darker) | ты | Ъ + и together — dark sound |
| Ь ь | soft sign (no sound) | жить | Makes prior consonant soft |
| Э э | "e" in *bed* | это | Backwards С with tongue |
| Ю ю | "yu" in *you* | юг | Looks like IO — "you" |
| Я я | "ya" in *yard* | яблоко | Backwards R — ya! |

## Vowel Pairs — Hard vs Soft

Russian vowels come in hard/soft pairs. Soft vowels palatalize the preceding consonant:

| Hard | Soft | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| А | Я | Я = "ya" |
| Э | Е | Е = "ye" |
| О | Ё | Ё = "yo" |
| У | Ю | Ю = "yu" |
| Ы | И | И = "ee" (soft) |

---

## Pronunciation Notes

### Vowel Reduction
Unstressed О is pronounced like А:
- *молоко* (milk) → [mɐlɐˈko] — the first two О's sound like А

### Voiced/Voiceless Consonants
At the end of a word, voiced consonants become voiceless:
- Б → П, В → Ф, Г → К, Д → Т, Ж → Ш, З → С
- *хлеб* (bread) sounds like /xlʲep/

## Tips for Slovak Speakers

- Slovak **C** = Russian **Ц** (ts sound)
- Slovak **Š** ≈ Russian **Ш**
- Slovak **Č** ≈ Russian **Ч**
- Slovak **Ž** ≈ Russian **Ж** (but more buzzy)
- The Cyrillic Р sounds like the Slovak rolled R — you already know this!
- Soft sign (Ь) is similar to the Slovak *ľ* softening concept
