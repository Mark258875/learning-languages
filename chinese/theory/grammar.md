# Chinese Grammar

## Overview

Mandarin grammar is actually simpler than French, Russian, or Slovak in many ways:
- **No verb conjugation** — verbs never change form
- **No noun gender** — no masculine/feminine
- **No case endings** — no declension
- **No plural inflection** — context and numbers indicate plural
- **Tones carry meaning** — but grammar structure is analytical (relies on word order)

---

## Basic Sentence Structure

**Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)** — same as English!

- *我 吃 饭。* — Wǒ chī fàn. — I eat rice.
- *她 喜欢 猫。* — Tā xǐhuān māo. — She likes cats.
- *我们 学习 中文。* — Wǒmen xuéxí zhōngwén. — We study Chinese.

---

## Pronouns

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 我 | wǒ | I / me |
| 你 | nǐ | you (informal) |
| 您 | nín | you (formal/polite) |
| 他 | tā | he / him |
| 她 | tā | she / her |
| 它 | tā | it |
| 我们 | wǒmen | we / us |
| 你们 | nǐmen | you (plural) |
| 他们/她们 | tāmen | they / them |

**Possession:** Add 的 (de) after pronoun
- 我的书 (wǒ de shū) — my book
- 他的猫 (tā de māo) — his/her cat

---

## The Particle 的 (de)

**的** is one of the most common characters. Key uses:

1. **Possession:** noun/pronoun + 的 + noun
   - 妈妈的书 — mom's book

2. **Adjective modifier:** adjective + 的 + noun
   - 好的书 — a good book
   - 漂亮的女孩 — a pretty girl

3. **After long modifiers** (always use 的):
   - 我昨天买的书 — the book I bought yesterday

## Measure Words (量词)

**Critical concept!** Chinese requires a measure word between a number and a noun:
Number + Measure Word + Noun

| Measure Word | Pinyin | Used for | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|---------|
| 个 | gè | general (people, things) | 一个人 (yī gè rén) one person |
| 本 | běn | books, volumes | 两本书 (liǎng běn shū) two books |
| 张 | zhāng | flat objects (paper, tickets, beds) | 一张纸 (yī zhāng zhǐ) one paper |
| 条 | tiáo | long/flexible objects (fish, roads, pants) | 一条鱼 (yī tiáo yú) one fish |
| 只 | zhī | small animals | 一只猫 (yī zhī māo) one cat |
| 杯 | bēi | cups/glasses | 一杯水 (yī bēi shuǐ) one glass of water |
| 碗 | wǎn | bowls | 一碗饭 (yī wǎn fàn) one bowl of rice |

> 两 (liǎng) is used instead of 二 (èr) before measure words: 两个人 (two people), not ~~二个人~~

## Negation

**不 (bù)** — negates verbs and adjectives (tone changes to bú before 4th tone)
- *我不吃肉。* — Wǒ bù chī ròu. — I don't eat meat.
- *她不是学生。* — Tā bú shì xuésheng. — She is not a student.

**没 (méi)** — negates 有 (to have) and past actions
- *我没有钱。* — Wǒ méiyǒu qián. — I don't have money.
- *我没吃早饭。* — Wǒ méi chī zǎofàn. — I didn't eat breakfast.

---

## Questions

**Yes/No questions:** Add **吗 (ma)** at the end
- *你是学生吗？* — Nǐ shì xuésheng ma? — Are you a student?

**Choice questions:** A + 还是 (háishì) + B
- *你喝茶还是咖啡？* — Do you drink tea or coffee?

**Question words:**
| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 谁 | shéi | who |
| 什么 | shénme | what |
| 哪里/哪儿 | nǎlǐ/nǎr | where |
| 什么时候 | shénme shíhou | when |
| 为什么 | wèishénme | why |
| 怎么 | zěnme | how |
| 多少 | duōshǎo | how many/much |
| 几 | jǐ | how many (small number) |

## Time Expressions

Time expressions come **before** the verb (opposite of English):
- *我明天去。* — Wǒ míngtiān qù. — I go tomorrow. (lit: I tomorrow go)
- *他每天学习。* — Tā měitiān xuéxí. — He studies every day.

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 今天 | jīntiān | today |
| 明天 | míngtiān | tomorrow |
| 昨天 | zuótiān | yesterday |
| 现在 | xiànzài | now |
| 每天 | měitiān | every day |
| 以前 | yǐqián | before/in the past |
| 以后 | yǐhòu | after/in the future |
