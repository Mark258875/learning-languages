# Chinese Tones (声调)

## Why Tones Matter

In Mandarin, **the same syllable with different tones = completely different words**.
There is no tone in Slovak, French, or English (as grammatical markers), so this requires conscious practice.

---

## The Four Tones + Neutral

### Visual Overview

```
Pitch:
High  5 ─────────────────
      4      /──    \
      3    /        \   /
      2  /            \ /
Low   1 ─────────────────
         1st    2nd   3rd  4th  neutral
          ─      /     ∨    \     ·
```

| Tone | Marker | Description | Analogy |
|------|--------|-------------|---------|
| 1st | ā | High level (pitch 5→5) | Singing a flat note "aaah" |
| 2nd | á | Rising (pitch 3→5) | Asking "What?" in surprise |
| 3rd | ǎ | Low dipping (pitch 2→1→4) | Saying "hmm" doubtfully |
| 4th | à | Falling (pitch 5→1) | Giving a sharp command "Stop!" |
| Neutral | a | Short, light, context-dependent | Unstressed syllable |

## The Classic Example: mā/má/mǎ/mà

| Pinyin | Tone | Character | Meaning |
|--------|------|-----------|---------|
| mā | 1st ─ | 妈 | mother |
| má | 2nd ↗ | 麻 | hemp / numb |
| mǎ | 3rd ↘↗ | 马 | horse |
| mà | 4th ↘ | 骂 | to scold |
| ma | neutral | 吗 | question particle |

Classic sentence: *妈妈骑马，马慢，妈妈骂马。*
*Māma qí mǎ, mǎ màn, māma mà mǎ.*
"Mom rides a horse; the horse is slow; mom scolds the horse."

## Third Tone Sandhi (Tone Change Rules)

The 3rd tone changes in certain contexts:

### Two 3rd Tones in a Row
When two 3rd tone syllables appear together, the first becomes 2nd tone:
- **nǐ hǎo** → pronounced **ní hǎo** (你好 hello)
- **wǒ yě** → pronounced **wó yě** (我也 me too)

### 3rd Tone Before Neutral Tone
3rd tone before neutral = half-3rd (only the falling part, no rise)

## Tips for Learning Tones

1. **Learn tones with words** — never learn a word without its tone
2. **Exaggerate at first** — make the tones dramatic, they'll naturalize
3. **Use tone marks** — always write pinyin with tone marks, never without
4. **Sing** — 1st tone is easy when you think of singing
5. **Practice minimal pairs** — mā/má/mǎ/mà daily
6. **Record yourself** — compare with native speaker audio

## Tone Marks on Vowels

The tone mark goes over the **main vowel** of the final:
- **Priority order:** a / e > o > i / u / ü
- If i and u appear together, the mark goes on the **second** one: *guì* (贵), *huì* (会)

| Word | Mark placement |
|------|---------------|
| bāo (包 bag) | over a |
| féi (肥 fat) | over e |
| liú (流 flow) | over u (second vowel) |
| guì (贵 expensive) | over i (second vowel) |
