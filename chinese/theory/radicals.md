# Chinese Radicals (部首)

## What are Radicals?

Chinese characters are not random — they are built from components called **radicals** (部首 bùshǒu). Learning radicals helps you:
- Guess the meaning of unknown characters
- Look up characters in a dictionary
- Remember characters more easily (they tell a story!)

Most characters combine a **semantic radical** (hints at meaning) and a **phonetic component** (hints at sound).

## Learning Strategy

1. **Learn the 50 most common radicals first** (this list)
2. **See radicals in characters** — don't just memorize strokes
3. **Story method**: 好 (good) = 女 (woman) + 子 (child) = having a woman and child = good!
4. **Group characters by radical** — learn 花,苹,茶 together (all have 艹 grass radical)

## Character Composition Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Pictograph | Looks like the thing | 日 (sun), 月 (moon), 山 (mountain) |
| Ideograph | Abstract idea | 上 (above), 下 (below), 一 (one) |
| Compound | Two meanings combined | 好 (woman + child = good) |
| Semantic-Phonetic | Radical (meaning) + phonetic (sound) | 清 qīng (氵water + 青 qīng sound) |

~90% of characters are semantic-phonetic compounds!
