# French Pronunciation

## The French Alphabet

French uses the same 26 letters as English, but pronunciation differs significantly.

---

## Key Sounds

### Nasal Vowels
These are unique to French — vowels pronounced through the nose:

| Written | Sound | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| -an / -am / -en / -em | /ɑ̃/ like "on" in "long" | *an*, *enfant*, *temps* |
| -in / -im / -ain / -aim / -ein | /ɛ̃/ like "an" in "can" (nasalized) | *vin*, *main*, *pain* |
| -on / -om | /ɔ̃/ like "on" in "bond" (nasalized) | *bon*, *son* |
| -un / -um | /œ̃/ (rare, merging with /ɛ̃/) | *un*, *parfum* |

> ⚠️ Nasals do NOT occur when followed by a double consonant or vowel: *bonne* = /bɔn/ (not nasal)

### The French R
The French /r/ is pronounced at the back of the throat (uvular trill/fricative).
- Practice: gargle lightly — that's roughly the sound
- *rouge*, *Paris*, *merci*

### The U sound (/y/)
Different from English "oo" — round your lips as if to say "oo" but say "ee":
- *lune* /lyn/ (moon)
- *rue* /ry/ (street)
- *vu* /vy/ (seen)

### EU / OEU (/ø/ and /œ/)
- *feu* /fø/ (fire) — like German ö
- *peur* /pœr/ (fear) — more open

## Silent Letters

**Final consonants are usually silent:**
- *chat* — the final *t* is silent → /ʃa/
- *grand* — the final *d* is silent → /ɡʁɑ̃/
- *vous parlez* — the final *z* is silent

**The letter H:**
- Always silent in French
- *h muet* (mute h): acts as if it isn't there → liaison occurs: *l'heure*
- *h aspiré* (aspirate h): no liaison, no elision: *le hibou* (not *l'hibou*)

**Final -e:**
- Usually silent: *femme* → /fam/, *grande* → /ɡʁɑ̃d/

---

## Liaison

When a normally silent final consonant is pronounced because the next word starts with a vowel or mute H:

- *les amis* → /lez‿ami/ (the friends)
- *vous avez* → /vuz‿ave/ (you have)
- *un enfant* → /œ̃n‿ɑ̃fɑ̃/ (a child)

**Mandatory liaison:** after determiners, subject pronouns + verb, etc.
**Forbidden liaison:** after *et* (and), after singular nouns, etc.

---

## Elision

Dropping the final vowel of short words before a vowel or mute H:

- *le + arbre* → *l'arbre* (the tree)
- *de + eau* → *de l'eau* (some water)
- *je + aime* → *j'aime* (I like)
- *ce + est* → *c'est* (it is)

## Accent Marks

| Accent | Example | Effect |
|--------|---------|--------|
| é (acute) | *été* | /e/ — closed "ay" sound |
| è (grave) | *mère* | /ɛ/ — open "eh" sound |
| ê (circumflex) | *forêt* | usually /ɛ/, historical marker |
| ë (diaeresis) | *Noël* | pronounce separately |
| ç (cedilla) | *garçon* | /s/ before a, o, u |
| â, î, ô, û | *château* | various, often subtle |

## Common Pitfalls

| Word | Wrong | Right |
|------|-------|-------|
| *plus* (more) | "ploos" | /plys/ |
| *plus* (no more, neg.) | "ploos" | /ply/ (silent s) |
| *beaucoup* | "boh-koop" | /boku/ |
| *fille* | "fill" | /fij/ |
| *grenouille* | "gren-oo-ill" | /ɡʁənuj/ |
