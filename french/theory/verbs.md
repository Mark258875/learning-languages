# French Verbs — Conjugation Reference

## Regular Verb Groups

French verbs fall into three regular groups based on their infinitive ending.

### Group 1: -ER Verbs (most common)
Model: **parler** (to speak)

| Pronoun | Present | Passé composé | Imparfait | Futur simple |
|---------|---------|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| je | parle | ai parlé | parlais | parlerai |
| tu | parles | as parlé | parlais | parleras |
| il/elle | parle | a parlé | parlait | parlera |
| nous | parlons | avons parlé | parlions | parlerons |
| vous | parlez | avez parlé | parliez | parlerez |
| ils/elles | parlent | ont parlé | parlaient | parleront |

### Group 2: -IR Verbs
Model: **finir** (to finish)

| Pronoun | Present | Passé composé | Imparfait | Futur simple |
|---------|---------|--------------|-----------|-------------|

## Passé Composé — Auxiliary Verb Choice

Most verbs use **avoir** as auxiliary. The following use **être** (MRS VANDERTRAMP):

**M**onter, **R**ester, **S**ortir · **V**enir, **A**ller, **N**aître · **D**escendre, **E**ntrer, **R**entrer · **T**omber, **R**etourner, **A**rriver · **M**ourir, **P**artir

Also: all **reflexive verbs** use être.

With être, the past participle agrees with the subject:
- *Elle est allée* (she went)
- *Ils sont partis* (they left)

---

## Reflexive Verbs

Use reflexive pronouns (me, te, se, nous, vous, se):

- *se lever* → *je me lève* (I get up)
- *se coucher* → *tu te couches* (you go to bed)
- *s'appeler* → *il s'appelle* (his name is)
