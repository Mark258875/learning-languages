# French Grammar

## Articles

### Definite Articles (the)
| | Masculine | Feminine | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Before consonant** | le | la | les |
| **Before vowel/h** | l' | l' | les |

**Examples:**
- *le chat* — the cat (m.)
- *la pomme* — the apple (f.)
- *l'arbre* — the tree (m., starts with vowel)
- *les enfants* — the children

### Indefinite Articles (a/an/some)
| | Masculine | Feminine | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| | un | une | des |

**Examples:**
- *un chien* — a dog
- *une fleur* — a flower
- *des livres* — some books

### Partitive Articles (some of / used with uncountable)
| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| | du | de la |
| **Before vowel** | de l' | de l' |

**Examples:**
- *Je mange du pain.* — I eat (some) bread.
- *Elle boit de l'eau.* — She drinks water.

## Gender

Every French noun is either masculine or feminine. Rules of thumb:

**Often Masculine:**
- Nouns ending in *-age*, *-ment*, *-eau*, *-isme*
- Days, months, seasons

**Often Feminine:**
- Nouns ending in *-tion*, *-sion*, *-ure*, *-ance*, *-ence*, *-ette*, *-ique*
- Most nouns ending in *-e*

> ⚠️ Exceptions exist — when in doubt, learn the gender with the word!

## Adjectives

Adjectives agree in gender and number with the noun they describe.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Masc. Plural | Fem. Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **grand** (tall) | grand | grande | grands | grandes |
| **petit** (small) | petit | petite | petits | petites |
| **beau** (beautiful) | beau | belle | beaux | belles |

Most adjectives follow the noun in French:
- *un livre intéressant* — an interesting book
- *une fille intelligente* — an intelligent girl

**BAGS adjectives** come *before* the noun (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size):
- *un beau jardin* — a beautiful garden
- *une vieille maison* — an old house
- *un petit chien* — a small dog

## Negation

Negation wraps around the conjugated verb: **ne … pas**

- *Je parle.* → *Je ne parle pas.* — I don't speak.
- *Il mange.* → *Il ne mange pas.* — He doesn't eat.

Before vowel, *ne* becomes *n'*:
- *J'aime* → *Je n'aime pas* — I don't like.

Other negations:
- *ne … jamais* — never
- *ne … rien* — nothing
- *ne … plus* — no longer
- *ne … que* — only

## Questions

**Yes/No questions:**
1. Rising intonation: *Tu parles français?*
2. Est-ce que: *Est-ce que tu parles français?*
3. Inversion: *Parles-tu français?* (formal)

**Question words:**
| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| Qui | Who |
| Quoi / Que | What |
| Où | Where |
| Quand | When |
| Pourquoi | Why |
| Comment | How |
| Combien | How much/many |
